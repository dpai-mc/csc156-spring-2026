üîÑThe Investigation Process

The digital forensics investigation process follows a systematic methodology to ensure evidence integrity and legal admissibility. Each phase builds upon the previous one, creating a comprehensive approach to forensic examination.

1

üîç Identification

Recognizing that an incident has occurred and determining the scope of investigation.

  • Recognize incident occurrence and severity
  • Determine investigation scope and boundaries
  • Identify potential evidence sources
  • Assess business impact and urgency
  • Notify stakeholders and determine authority
  • Document initial observations
2

üõ°Ô∏è Preservation

Securing evidence and preventing contamination or destruction.

  • Secure physical and digital scene
  • Prevent evidence contamination
  • Implement write protection measures
  • Isolate affected systems safely
  • Document evidence state at discovery
  • Consider order of volatility
3

üíæ Collection/Acquisition

Creating forensic images and gathering evidence using proper procedures.

  • Create forensic images with write blockers
  • Collect volatile data (RAM, network state)
  • Gather log files and system artifacts
  • Compute cryptographic hashes (MD5, SHA-256)
  • Document collection procedures thoroughly
  • Package and transport evidence securely
4

üî¨ Examination

Processing forensic images and extracting relevant artifacts.

  • Process images with forensic tools
  • Extract relevant artifacts and data
  • Recover deleted files and partitions
  • Decrypt or decode protected data
  • Identify files of interest
  • Run automated analysis tools
5

üìä Analysis

Interpreting examination results and reconstructing events.

  • Interpret examination findings
  • Reconstruct event timelines
  • Determine significance of evidence
  • Test hypotheses about what occurred
  • Identify patterns and anomalies
  • Draw evidence-based conclusions
6

üìù Presentation/Reporting

Documenting findings and communicating results to stakeholders.

  • Document findings in comprehensive reports
  • Create executive summaries
  • Prepare evidence for legal proceedings
  • Present findings to diverse audiences
  • Provide expert testimony if required
  • Archive evidence and documentation

üî¨ Lab Activity 2: Chain of Custody Documentation Exercise (20 points)

‚ñº

Objective: Practice proper evidence handling and chain of custody documentation using a simulated investigation scenario.

Scenario:

You're a forensic examiner at Mendocino College IT Security. On January 28, 2026, at 10:30 AM, the IT Director reports that employee Sarah Chen's laptop may contain evidence of unauthorized data access. The laptop (Dell Latitude 5420, Asset #MC-5678, Serial #ABCD1234) is currently powered off on Sarah's desk in Building B, Room 305.

You arrive at the scene at 11:00 AM with IT Director Michael Johnson (who will serve as witness). You photograph the laptop, document its condition (good condition, no damage, "Sarah's Laptop" label on lid), remove it from the desk, and place it in an anti-static evidence bag. You seal the bag with tamper-evident seal #789. You transport the laptop to the Forensic Lab (Building A, Room 150) and place it in Evidence Locker #5 at 11:30 AM.

Tasks:

  1. Review provided investigation scenario
  2. Complete chain of custody forms for multiple evidence items
  3. Document evidence collection procedures
  4. Create investigation timeline
  5. Identify potential chain of custody weaknesses
  6. Submit completed documentation package

Deliverables:

  • Completed chain of custody forms (template provided)
  • Evidence collection log
  • Photographic documentation log
  • Written reflection on challenges encountered (250-500 words)

Grading Criteria:

  • Completeness of documentation (8 points): All required fields filled in accurately
  • Accuracy of chain of custody forms (6 points): Correct dates, times, locations, and procedures
  • Proper use of forensic terminology (3 points): Professional language and correct technical terms
  • Quality of reflection and analysis (3 points): Thoughtful consideration of challenges and solutions

  • Use the chain of custody form template provided in Canvas
  • Pay attention to detail‚Äîmissing information can invalidate evidence
