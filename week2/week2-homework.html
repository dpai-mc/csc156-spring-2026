Instructions

You are a forensic examiner at Mendocino College IT Security. Complete the field notes form below based on the scenario provided. Your documentation must be thorough, accurate, and follow professional forensic standards.

Important: Field notes are legal documents. They must be contemporaneous (created at the time of observation), complete, accurate, and objective. Your notes may be used in court proceedings.

Scenario: Unauthorized Network Access Investigation

Date: Thursday, January 29, 2026

Initial Report Time: 09:15 AM

Your Role: Digital Forensics Examiner

Background:

The Network Security Manager, Robert Martinez, contacts you at 09:15 AM reporting suspicious network activity. System logs show that on January 28, 2026, between 11:45 PM and 2:30 AM, someone accessed the student records database from employee Marcus Thompson's workstation (Computer ID: MC-LAB-042). Marcus was not on campus during this time—he was attending a conference in San Francisco and has hotel receipts to prove it.

Your Actions:

  • You arrive at the Computer Lab (Building C, Room 215) at 09:45 AM with Robert Martinez as witness
  • The workstation is powered on, showing the Windows login screen
  • You photograph the workstation in place (4 photos taken)
  • You note the workstation details: Dell OptiPlex 7090, Asset Tag MC-LAB-042, Serial Number 7XKMP3
  • You observe the keyboard has visible fingerprint residue on several keys
  • You document the network cable is connected to port 15 on switch SW-C215-01
  • You photograph the screen and surrounding area
  • You properly shut down the computer following forensic procedures
  • You disconnect the network cable and label it with evidence tag ET-2026-0129-001
  • You place the computer tower in an anti-static bag and seal with tamper-evident seal #456
  • You transport the workstation to the Forensic Lab (Building A, Room 150) at 10:30 AM
  • You place it in Evidence Locker #3 and secure the locker

Key Elements of Professional Field Notes:
  • Contemporaneous: Written at the time of observation
  • Chronological: Events recorded in time order with timestamps
  • Complete: All relevant details documented
  • Accurate: Factual, objective observations only
  • Clear: Unambiguous language, specific details
  • Permanent: Written in ink (or electronic equivalent), no erasures
