Option 1: Ethical Dilemma - Conflicting Loyalties

Scenario:

You're a forensic examiner working for a law firm representing a corporation in a civil lawsuit. During your examination of email evidence, you discover communications that strongly suggest your client (the corporation) engaged in illegal activity unrelated to the current lawsuit‚Äîspecifically, systematic tax fraud.

Your supervising attorney instructs you to "focus only on the lawsuit matters" and not include the tax fraud evidence in your report. However, you know that the tax fraud evidence is potentially relevant to an ongoing federal investigation (though you're not directly involved in that investigation).

Discuss:

  • What ethical principles are in conflict?
  • What do professional ethics codes (IACIS, (ISC)¬≤, HTCIA) say about this situation?
  • What are your ethical obligations?
  • What would you do and why?
  • What are the potential consequences of different actions?

Option 2: Privacy vs. Thoroughness

Scenario:

During a corporate investigation of suspected intellectual property theft, you're examining an employee's work laptop. The employee's acceptable use policy acknowledges that company devices may be monitored.

While searching for evidence of IP theft, you discover:

  • Personal medical records indicating a serious illness
  • Intimate personal photographs
  • Communications with an attorney about discrimination claims against the company
  • Evidence suggesting the employee is a whistleblower reporting safety violations

None of this information appears directly relevant to the IP theft investigation, but your employer wants "everything" documented.

Discuss:

  • How do you balance thoroughness with privacy respect?
  • What ethical principles guide your decisions?
  • What should be included/excluded from your report?
  • How do you handle attorney-client privileged communications?
  • What if excluding information helps the employer retaliate against the whistleblower?

Option 3: Professional Ethics Code Comparison

Task:

Compare the ethics codes of TWO professional organizations from this list:

  • IACIS (International Association of Computer Investigative Specialists)
  • (ISC)¬≤ (International Information System Security Certification Consortium)
  • HTCIA (High Technology Crime Investigation Association)
  • EC-Council (International Council of E-Commerce Consultants - CHFI code)

Discuss:

  • What common principles appear in both codes?
  • What differences in emphasis or approach exist?
  • Which code do you find most comprehensive and why?
  • How would these codes guide your professional practice?
  • Provide specific examples of how you would apply principles from these codes

Note: You'll need to research and read the actual ethics codes from these organizations (available on their websites).
