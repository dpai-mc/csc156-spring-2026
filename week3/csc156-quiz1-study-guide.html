Week 2: Investigation Process (8 questions, 10 points)

Key Topics to Review:

  • Six phases of digital forensics investigation lifecycle:
    1. Identification
    2. Preservation
    3. Collection
    4. Examination
    5. Analysis
    6. Presentation
  • Purpose and activities in each phase
  • Difference between examination and analysis
  • First responder responsibilities
  • Evidence handling procedures
  • Documentation requirements throughout investigation

üí° Sample Question

Which of the following is NOT one of the six phases of the digital forensics investigation lifecycle?

A) Identification
B) Preservation
C) Prosecution
D) Analysis

Answer: C) Prosecution (not part of the six-phase lifecycle)

Week 2: Chain of Custody (4 questions, 5 points)

Key Topics to Review:

  • Definition and purpose of chain of custody
  • Required information in chain of custody documentation:
    • Who collected evidence
    • When and where collected
    • Who handled evidence
    • What was done with evidence
    • Transfer dates and signatures
  • Consequences of broken chain of custody
  • Best practices for maintaining custody
  • Evidence storage and handling procedures

üí° Sample Question

Scenario: An investigator creates a forensic image of a suspect's hard drive but forgets to document the hash values. Later, the defense attorney challenges the integrity of the image. What is the likely result?

A) The image will be admitted because the investigator is trustworthy
B) The image may be challenged or excluded due to lack of authentication
C) Hash values are optional for forensic images
D) The defense cannot challenge properly collected evidence

Answer: B) Without hash values, the examiner cannot prove the image wasn't altered, weakening authentication under FRE 901.

Week 3: Legal Principles (8 questions, 10 points)

Key Topics to Review:

  • Federal Rules of Evidence:
    • Rule 401: Relevant Evidence
    • Rule 403: Excluding Relevant Evidence
    • Rule 702: Expert Testimony
    • Rule 802: Hearsay Rule
    • Rule 803(6): Business Records Exception
    • Rule 901: Authentication
  • Daubert Standard: Five factors for expert testimony
    1. Testing
    2. Peer Review
    3. Error Rate
    4. Standards
    5. General Acceptance
  • Fourth Amendment:
    • Reasonable expectation of privacy test
    • Warrant requirements
    • Exceptions (consent, exigent circumstances, plain view)
    • Private party searches
  • Landmark Cases:
    • Riley v. California (cell phone warrants)
    • Carpenter v. United States (location data)
  • ECPA: Three titles (Wiretap Act, SCA, Pen/Trap)

üí° Sample Question

Under the Daubert Standard, which of the following is NOT one of the five factors courts consider when determining if scientific expert testimony is admissible?

A) Whether the theory has been tested
B) The known or potential error rate
C) The cost-effectiveness of the method
D) General acceptance in the scientific community

Answer: C) Cost-effectiveness is not a Daubert factor

Key Topics to Review:

  • Six Core Ethical Principles:
    1. Objectivity
    2. Integrity
    3. Competence
    4. Confidentiality
    5. Accountability
    6. Legal Compliance
  • Professional Ethics Codes: IACIS, (ISC)¬≤, HTCIA, ISFCE
  • Privacy Laws:
    • GDPR (when it applies to US companies)
    • CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)
    • HIPAA (health information)
    • COPPA (children's privacy)
  • Common ethical dilemmas and resolutions
  • Ethical decision-making framework

üí° Sample Question

Which ethical principle is violated when a forensic examiner reports only evidence that supports the prosecution's theory while omitting exculpatory evidence?

A) Competence
B) Confidentiality
C) Objectivity
D) Accountability

Answer: C) Objectivity requires reporting all relevant findings without bias

üìù Sample Questions

Practice with these sample questions to prepare for Quiz 1:

Sample Question 1: Multiple Choice

Which Federal Rule of Evidence provides an exception to the hearsay rule for business records, allowing computer-generated records to be admitted in court?

A) Rule 401
B) Rule 702
C) Rule 803(6)
D) Rule 901

Answer: C) Rule 803(6) - Business Records Exception

Sample Question 2: True/False

True or False: The Fourth Amendment restricts both government actors and private employers from conducting searches without warrants.

Answer: FALSE - Fourth Amendment only restricts government, not private parties (unless private party acts as government agent)

Sample Question 3: Short Answer

Explain why hash values (like SHA-256) are important in digital forensics. (2-3 sentences)

Sample Answer: Hash values serve as cryptographic "fingerprints" that prove evidence integrity. By calculating a hash at the time of collection and again before analysis or in court, examiners can demonstrate that evidence has not been altered. Matching hash values authenticate the evidence under FRE Rule 901 and establish chain of custody.

Sample Question 4: Scenario-Based

Scenario: A corporate forensic examiner discovers that an employee being investigated for policy violations has child exploitation material on their work computer. What should the examiner do?

A) Continue the investigation and include it in the report to the employer
B) Ignore it since it's outside the investigation scope
C) Immediately stop the examination and report to law enforcement and supervisor
D) Complete the policy investigation first, then decide whether to report

Answer: C) Child exploitation material must be immediately reported to law enforcement. This is both a legal requirement (mandatory reporting) and ethical obligation.
