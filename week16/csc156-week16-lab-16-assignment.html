Hints & Common Pitfalls

Hash Verification Mistakes

Always compute hashes before mounting or opening evidence. Use write-blockers or mount as read-only. If a hash changes, stop immediately and document the discrepancy.

Chain of Custody Gaps

Every action — even viewing a file — should be logged. Gaps in your chain of custody can undermine your entire investigation. Set a timer or use a script to remind yourself to log actions.

Confirmation Bias

Avoid tunnel vision. Seek evidence that disproves your hypothesis, not just evidence that confirms it. Document exculpatory findings alongside incriminating ones.

Tool Output Without Interpretation

Raw tool output is not analysis. Always explain what an artifact means in context, why it is significant, and how it relates to your key questions.

Timeline Synchronization

Different evidence sources may use different time zones or clock skews. Normalize all timestamps to a single reference (typically UTC) and note any offsets.

Scope Creep

Stay within your defined scope. If you discover evidence outside your authorization, document it and consult your instructor — do not analyze it without permission.
