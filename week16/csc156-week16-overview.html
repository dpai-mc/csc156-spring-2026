Week 16 Overview – Capstone Project Work and Course Integration

CSC 156 – Digital Forensic Fundamentals

⚠ This Is Your Most Important Week

The Final Project (Capstone) is the single largest graded deliverable in this course — 100 points. Week 16 is your last full work week before final submission and presentations in Week 17. Use every available hour this week to execute your investigation, draft your report, and verify your work.

What This Week Is About

Welcome to Week 16. There is no new technical content this week — and that is intentional. This entire week is dedicated to doing the work: executing your capstone forensic investigation, integrating evidence from multiple domains, and drafting a professional report that demonstrates everything you have learned this semester.

Think of this week as the moment in a real-world engagement when the forensic examiner moves from learning and planning into focused execution. You have spent fifteen weeks building your toolkit — file system analysis, OS artifacts, network forensics, mobile forensics, malware analysis, memory forensics, cloud forensics, reporting, and incident response frameworks. Now you bring all of those skills together in a single, cohesive investigation.

Your instructor is available for consultation, but you are driving the investigation. This mirrors professional practice: manage your workflow, verify your evidence, document your methodology, and communicate your findings clearly.

Week 16 Learning Goals

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  1. Execute a multi-phase forensic investigation applying acquisition, analysis, and reporting skills to a realistic scenario.
  2. Integrate techniques from multiple forensic domains (file systems, OS artifacts, network, mobile, malware) within a single investigation.
  3. Maintain a complete, court-admissible chain of custody and evidence log throughout your capstone investigation.
  4. Draft a professional forensic report that documents methodology, findings, and conclusions with appropriate supporting exhibits.
  5. Apply structured quality-assurance review to your own work and provide constructive peer feedback on another student's methodology.
  6. Demonstrate ethical decision-making when encountering ambiguous or sensitive evidence during an investigation.

Looking Ahead – Week 17

Week 17 brings final project submission, peer review presentations, and course wrap-up. The work you do this week determines how polished and confident your final deliverable will be — so invest the time now.
