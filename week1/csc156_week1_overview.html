Week Overview

This first week introduces students to the field of digital forensics as a profession, establishing foundational knowledge about the role of forensics in cybersecurity and incident response. Students will become familiar with the course structure, lab environment, and the various professional certifications available in the field.

Learning Objectives

By the end of Week 1, students will be able to:

  1. Define digital forensics and explain its role in modern cybersecurity
  2. Identify career paths and professional certifications in digital forensics
  3. Describe the relationship between incident response and forensic investigation
  4. Navigate the course lab environment and forensic tools
  5. Explain the scope and expectations of the course

Topics Covered This Week

Lecture Topics:

  • Overview of Digital Forensics as a Profession - Understanding the field, its history, and its evolution
  • Role of Digital Forensics in Incident Response and Cybersecurity - How forensics fits into the broader security landscape
  • Industry Certifications Overview - IACIS CFCE, GIAC GCFE, GIAC GCFA, and EC-Council CHFI
  • Course Structure and Expectations - Syllabus review, grading policies, and success strategies

Lab Activities:

  • Lab Environment Setup - Installing and configuring forensic workstation tools
  • Tool Familiarization - Introduction to Autopsy, FTK Imager, and other core forensic tools
  • Navigation Exercises - Learning to navigate the lab environment efficiently
  • Documentation Practice - Beginning your forensic notebook and proper documentation techniques
