Week 15 | Lab 14 – Incident Response Tabletop and Triage Exercise

30 Points

Lab Purpose

This tabletop exercise tests your ability to apply incident response frameworks, select appropriate forensic actions, prioritize evidence collection under time constraints, and develop an IR plan. Unlike hands-on tool labs, this assessment evaluates your decision-making, process knowledge, and planning skills — the same competencies tested in professional DFIR interviews and real-world IR engagements. You will work through a multi-phase incident at MedTech Health Systems, responding as the forensic examiner on call.

⚠️ Scenario Context — MedTech Health Systems

MedTech Health Systems is a mid-size healthcare organization (1,200 employees, 3 hospital campuses) that manages electronic health records (EHR), medical imaging data, and insurance billing systems. Their environment includes:

  • MEDDB01 – Primary patient records database server (Linux, PostgreSQL)
  • WEBPORTAL – Patient-facing web portal (Windows Server 2019, IIS)
  • DC-MAIN – Active Directory domain controller (Windows Server 2022)
  • WKS-RAD04 – Radiology workstation (Windows 11, DICOM viewer)
  • EXCH-01 – Exchange email server (Windows Server 2019)

At 3:14 AM on Tuesday, March 17, 2026, the SOC receives an alert: MEDDB01 is making outbound connections to IP address 185.47.xx.xx (geolocated to Eastern Europe). The IR team is activated. You are the forensic examiner on call.

Task 1 – IR Phase Identification

6 Points (1 pt each)
Instructions: Below are six scenario snapshots at different moments in the MedTech incident timeline. For each, identify the current IR phase using either the NIST SP 800-61 framework (Preparation → Detection & Analysis → Containment, Eradication & Recovery → Post-Incident Activity) or the SANS PICERL model (Preparation → Identification → Containment → Eradication → Recovery → Lessons Learned). State the phase name and write 2–3 sentences justifying your choice.
Snapshot 1 of 6
The SOC analyst reviews the SIEM alert showing MEDDB01 initiating TLS connections to 185.47.xx.xx on port 443 every 90 seconds. The analyst correlates this with a failed login spike on the same server 6 hours earlier. They page the IR Manager and document the initial indicators of compromise (IOCs) in the ticketing system.
Snapshot 2 of 6
Two weeks prior to this incident, the IR Manager conducted a review of the organization's IR playbooks, confirmed forensic toolkit readiness (write-blockers charged, FTK Imager on USB), and updated the emergency contact list for legal counsel and the HIPAA privacy officer.
Snapshot 3 of 6
The network team implements a firewall rule blocking all traffic to/from 185.47.xx.xx. Simultaneously, the IR team decides NOT to power off MEDDB01 yet — they want to capture volatile memory first. They isolate the server's VLAN from the rest of the network while keeping it powered on.
Snapshot 4 of 6
After acquiring memory and disk images from MEDDB01, the forensic examiner identifies a web shell (cmd.aspx) on WEBPORTAL that was used as the initial access point. The sysadmin removes the web shell, patches the exploited vulnerability (CVE-2025-21987), and resets all service account credentials that had access to both servers.
Snapshot 5 of 6
The database team restores MEDDB01 from a verified clean backup dated March 10. They apply all security patches, harden the PostgreSQL configuration, enable enhanced audit logging, and conduct validation testing before reconnecting the server to the production VLAN. The IR Manager monitors for 48 hours to confirm no recurrence.
Snapshot 6 of 6
Three weeks later, the full IR team, legal counsel, and the CISO meet to review the incident timeline, evaluate what worked and what didn't, update the IR playbook to include healthcare-specific exfiltration scenarios, and schedule quarterly tabletop exercises. The forensic examiner presents a lessons-learned brief on evidence handling gaps identified during the engagement.

Task 2 – Forensic Action Selection

8 Points (2 pts each)
Instructions: At each decision point below, describe the specific forensic actions you would take and explain your rationale. You must reference specific tools and artifact names (e.g., "Use Volatility's malfind plugin to detect injected code in the process memory space"). Each response should be 4–6 sentences.
Decision Point 1 of 4
You arrive at MedTech's data center at 4:22 AM. MEDDB01 is still running and network-isolated. The IR Manager says: "We need to know what's in memory before we do anything else. We also need to preserve the evidence legally — this may become a HIPAA breach notification case." What forensic actions do you take first, and in what order?
Decision Point 2 of 4
Memory analysis reveals a suspicious process named svchost_update.exe (PID 4872) with network connections to 185.47.xx.xx. The process is injecting into lsass.exe. You also notice the process was launched from C:\ProgramData\temp\ on WEBPORTAL (the web server), suggesting lateral movement. What forensic actions do you perform to trace the attack path?
Decision Point 3 of 4
Log analysis reveals that the attacker used compromised credentials for the service account svc_dbbackup to move from WEBPORTAL to MEDDB01 via SSH. The account has access to four other servers. The IR Manager asks: "Do we have evidence the attacker accessed any other systems?" How do you investigate lateral movement scope?
Decision Point 4 of 4
The forensic timeline shows that between 2:00 AM and 3:14 AM, approximately 4.2 GB of data was transferred outbound from MEDDB01. The organization needs to determine whether protected health information (PHI) was exfiltrated. You have a full PCAP capture from the network tap for that time window. What do you analyze, and what artifacts help you determine data content?

Task 3 – Triage Prioritization

8 Points
Instructions: It is now 5:00 AM. You are the only forensic examiner available until the second examiner arrives at 8:00 AM (3 hours). The IR Manager confirms that four systems require forensic attention. You cannot image all four in time. Using the severity / scope / volatility framework from Module 15.3, create a prioritized evidence collection plan. For each system, assign a priority rank (1 = first), state what evidence you will collect, and justify your ordering in 3–4 sentences.

Situational Constraints:

  • You have one forensic workstation, one write-blocker, and a RAM capture tool.
  • Full disk images take ~45 minutes per server; RAM capture takes ~15 minutes.
  • MEDDB01 is network-isolated but still powered on (volatile data at risk if power fails).
  • WEBPORTAL was rebooted by a panicked sysadmin at 4:45 AM (volatile data lost).
  • DC-MAIN shows no alerts but the compromised account has Domain Admin privileges.
  • WKS-RAD04 has a scheduled auto-update reboot at 6:00 AM (1 hour from now).
System A: MEDDB01 (Patient Records Database Server)

Linux | PostgreSQL | Confirmed C2 beaconing | Network-isolated, powered on

System B: WEBPORTAL (Patient Web Portal)

Windows Server 2019 | IIS | Initial access point (web shell) | Rebooted at 4:45 AM

System C: DC-MAIN (Domain Controller)

Windows Server 2022 | AD DS | No alerts, but compromised account has DA privileges

System D: WKS-RAD04 (Radiology Workstation)

Windows 11 | DICOM viewer | Auto-update reboot scheduled at 6:00 AM | No confirmed compromise

Task 4 – IR Plan Outline

8 Points
Instructions: Based on what you have learned from this incident, develop a concise Incident Response Plan outline for MedTech Health Systems. This plan should be forward-looking — it is the document the organization will use for future incidents. Address each section below using the template structure from Module 15.3. Write in professional language suitable for an organizational policy document. Each section should be 4–8 sentences or equivalent bullet points.
Define at least 4 IR team roles, their responsibilities, and escalation authority. Include the forensic examiner's specific responsibilities and reporting relationships.
Define at least 3 severity levels with examples relevant to a healthcare organization. Include criteria for HIPAA breach determination and mandatory notification triggers.
Outline the standard forensic acquisition workflow (order of volatility, chain of custody, imaging procedures). Specify tools approved for use and documentation requirements.
Define internal notification chains, external communication requirements (legal, regulatory, law enforcement, patients), and rules about public disclosure. Address HIPAA's 60-day notification requirement.
Define the specific conditions that must be met before affected systems are returned to production. Include monitoring requirements, validation testing, and sign-off authority.

📚 Quick Reference

NIST SP 800-61 Phases

  • 1. Preparation – Establishing IR capability
  • 2. Detection & Analysis – Identifying and validating incidents
  • 3. Containment, Eradication & Recovery – Limiting damage, removing threats, restoring
  • 4. Post-Incident Activity – Lessons learned, documentation

SANS PICERL Phases

  • P – Preparation
  • I – Identification
  • C – Containment
  • E – Eradication
  • R – Recovery
  • L – Lessons Learned

Order of Volatility (RFC 3227)

PrioritySourceVolatility
1CPU registers, cacheHighest
2RAM (memory)High
3Network state / connectionsHigh
4Running processesHigh
5Disk (file system)Medium
6Remote logging / monitoringLow
7Archival media / backupsLowest

Three-Factor Prioritization Matrix

  • Severity – How critical is the compromised data/system?
  • Scope – How many systems/users are affected?
  • Volatility – How quickly will the evidence disappear?

Common Forensic Tools Reference

  • Memory: FTK Imager (RAM capture), Volatility 3, Rekall
  • Disk: FTK Imager, dd/dc3dd, Autopsy, EnCase
  • Network: Wireshark, NetworkMiner, Zeek
  • Logs: Event Viewer, Log2Timeline/Plaso, ELK Stack
  • Timeline: Plaso/log2timeline, Autopsy timeline
  • Registry: RegRipper, Registry Explorer
  • Hashing: md5sum, sha256sum, HashCalc

IR Plan Required Sections

  • Roles & Responsibilities
  • Incident Classification / Severity Levels
  • Forensic Acquisition Procedures
  • Communication Plan
  • Recovery Criteria
