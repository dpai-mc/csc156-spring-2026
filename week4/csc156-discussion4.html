üí¨ Discussion Overview

This week's discussion focuses on the critical differences between Hard Disk Drives (HDDs) and Solid State Drives (SSDs) and their profound impact on digital forensic investigations. This is an opportunity to apply your knowledge from Modules 4.1-4.4 and engage with your classmates on these evolving challenges.

Objective:

To demonstrate understanding of HDD and SSD technologies and their forensic implications, and to engage in thoughtful peer discussion.

Total Points: 3 points

CSLO Alignment: CSLO 2

This discussion supports CSLO 2: Conduct thorough examinations of computer hard disk drives, and other electronic data storage media to recover information and electronic data. Your ability to articulate the challenges posed by different storage media is key to conducting effective examinations.

üìù Part 1: Initial Post (2 points)

Choose **ONE** of the following discussion prompts. Your initial post should be a well-researched and thoughtful response.

Option 1: SSD Data Recovery Challenges

Research a real-world case where SSD technology significantly complicated a digital forensic investigation, particularly regarding deleted file recovery. Describe the case briefly, explain *how* SSD characteristics (TRIM, wear leveling, garbage collection) impacted the investigation, and what the ultimate outcome was for evidence recovery.

Option 2: TRIM and Evidence Destruction

Explain the technical function of the TRIM command and how it affects digital forensic investigations, especially concerning deleted data. Discuss the ethical and legal trade-offs forensic examiners face when dealing with TRIM-enabled SSDs (e.g., balancing evidence preservation with data destruction for performance).

Option 3: Hardware Encryption Dilemma

Many modern SSDs are Self-Encrypting Drives (SEDs) with hardware encryption. Discuss the legal, technical, and ethical considerations when a suspect's SED is locked and the password is unknown. How does this compare to software encryption on an HDD?

Option 4: Future of Storage Forensics

Based on current trends (e.g., persistent memory, cloud storage, new NAND technologies), how do you anticipate storage technologies will continue to impact digital forensics in the next 5-10 years? What new challenges and opportunities do you foresee?
