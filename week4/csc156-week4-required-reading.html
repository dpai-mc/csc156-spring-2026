üìñ Reading Overview

This week's required reading provides the foundational knowledge necessary to succeed in Lab Activity 4 and participate effectively in Discussion Post 4. It reinforces and expands upon the concepts introduced in Modules 4.1-4.4.

Objective:

To acquire foundational knowledge of computer hardware, storage technologies, interfaces, and boot processes, and to understand their forensic implications.

Total Points: 0 points (Required for success)

CSLO Alignment: CSLO 2

This reading directly supports CSLO 2: Conduct thorough examinations of computer hard disk drives, and other electronic data storage media to recover information and electronic data. A strong understanding of hardware is a prerequisite for effective examination.

üìö Required Reading List

Please complete both of the following readings. It is highly recommended to complete these before attempting Lab Activity 4.

Required readings (read both articles):

  • URL: https://www.forensicfocus.com/articles/recovering-evidence-from-ssd-drives-in-2014-understanding-trim-garbage-collection-and-exclusions/

    Focus: How SSDs handle deleted data, the role of TRIM and garbage collection, and why this makes traditional data recovery unreliable on solid-state drives.

  • ACM page (if you have library access): https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1368506.1368519

    Open PDF mirror: https://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/document?doi=e6c3b581cae4cef8ec60204d35a3014fb4868130&repid=rep1&type=pdf

    Focus: How full-disk (including self-encrypting) drives change forensic strategy, especially the importance of seizing systems while powered and handling encryption keys.

üéØ Reading Goals

As you read, focus on answering these questions:

  • What are the fundamental differences in how HDDs and SSDs store and manage data?
  • How do TRIM, wear leveling, and garbage collection directly impact the recoverability of deleted files on SSDs?
  • What are the key characteristics and forensic considerations for SATA, NVMe, and USB interfaces?
  • How do BIOS and UEFI differ in their boot processes, and what is the role of the EFI System Partition (ESP)?
  • What are the primary advantages of GPT over MBR partitioning, and what forensic artifacts can be found in each?
  • Why is careful documentation of RAID configurations absolutely critical?
Connect to Previous Learning:

Think about how the technical details you're reading about connect to the investigation process (Week 2) and legal admissibility (Week 3). How does understanding hardware help you preserve evidence and ensure its integrity?
