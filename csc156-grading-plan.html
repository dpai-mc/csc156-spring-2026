Final Project Details

Total Value: 120 points (12% of final grade) | Due: Week 17, Day 5

Project Overview

The final project is a comprehensive forensic investigation that demonstrates your ability to conduct a professional digital forensics examination and present findings in a formal report and presentation.

Project Components

1. Written Report (100 points)

Due: Week 17, Day 5 (before presentation)
Format: Professional forensic investigation report (15-25 pages)
Submission: PDF format via course management system

Section Points Requirements
Executive Summary 10 Clear overview of case and key findings
Case Background 10 Context, scope, objectives, legal authorization
Methodology 15 Tools used, procedures followed, chain of custody
Technical Findings 30 Detailed analysis with evidence artifacts
Timeline Reconstruction 15 Chronological events with supporting evidence
Conclusions 10 Summary of findings and recommendations
Documentation Quality 10 Professional formatting, grammar, citations

2. Oral Presentation (20 points)

Duration: 10-12 minutes
Format: Slide presentation (PowerPoint, Google Slides, etc.)
Timing: Week 17, Day 5 (in-class presentations)

Component Points Description
Content Quality 8 Clear, accurate summary of investigation
Presentation Skills 6 Professional delivery, eye contact, clarity
Visual Aids 4 Effective use of slides, diagrams, screenshots
Time Management 2 Stays within time limit

Project Options

Students may choose from:

  1. Provided forensic image analysis (recommended) - Analyze a provided disk image or memory dump
  2. Custom scenario with instructor approval - Create your own investigation scenario
  3. Forensic CTF participation - Participate in online forensic CTF with formal report

Course Policies

Attendance

Regular attendance is essential for success in this course. Lab activities cannot be made up except in cases of documented emergencies or illness. Missing more than 3 labs without valid documentation may result in a failing grade.

Late Work Policy

Assignment Type Late Penalty Maximum Extension
Lab Activities 10% per day late 3 days
Homework 10% per day late 5 days
Quizzes No makeup without documentation Contact instructor within 24 hours
Discussion Posts 50% if late 2 days
Final Project 20% per day late 2 days
Exams No makeup without documentation Schedule makeup within 1 week
Note: No work accepted after the final exam date without exceptional circumstances.

Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be your own. Collaboration is encouraged on labs and discussions but written submissions must be individual unless specifically designated as group work. Use of AI tools for code generation or analysis is permitted but must be documented and cited. Copying forensic reports or presenting others' analysis as your own constitutes academic dishonesty.

Plagiarism Consequences

  • First offense: Zero on assignment + academic integrity meeting
  • Second offense: Failing grade in course + referral to college administration

Collaboration Guidelines

  • Encouraged: Discussing concepts, troubleshooting tools, comparing approaches
  • Permitted: Helping classmates understand errors or techniques
  • Prohibited: Copying lab reports, sharing quiz answers, submitting identical work

Tool Usage

Students are encouraged to use any forensic tools that assist learning. Document all tools and techniques used in lab reports. Maintain forensic integrity—never modify evidence, always use write protection, document all actions.

Accessibility and Accommodations

Students with documented disabilities should contact the instructor and Disabled Student Programs and Services (DSPS) within the first two weeks to arrange appropriate accommodations.

Tips for Success

Stay Organized

  • Keep detailed lab notebooks with screenshots and observations
  • Maintain organized folders for forensic images and evidence
  • Track assignment due dates using course calendar
  • Back up all work regularly

Develop Professional Habits

  • Document everything—assume your work may be used in court
  • Practice writing clear, concise technical reports
  • Learn to explain technical findings to non-technical audiences
  • Follow chain of custody procedures even in practice scenarios

Master the Tools

  • Spend extra time with Autopsy, Volatility, Wireshark, and other core tools
  • Practice with additional datasets beyond assigned work
  • Join online forensics communities (Reddit r/computerforensics, DFIR Discord)
  • Review tool documentation and tutorials

Engage with the Material

  • Participate actively in discussions
  • Ask questions in lab and lecture
  • Connect concepts across different forensic domains
  • Follow current forensic cases and techniques in the news

Prepare for Certification

  • Keep certification objectives in mind while studying
  • Review practice exams for CFCE, CHFI, GCFE, GCFA
  • Build a study group for certification preparation
  • Consider taking a certification exam after course completion
